# Scope and Boundaries

This document defines what belongs in the FRACTALS knowledge base and what does not.

---

## In Scope

The following are explicitly in scope:

1. Named fractal sets and families (Mandelbrot, Julia, Cantor, Koch, Sierpinski, Barnsley, etc.)
2. Generation methods that produce fractal-dimension outputs (escape-time, IFS, L-systems, recursive geometry, stochastic methods, Newton iteration, DLA)
3. Mathematical properties of fractal structures (Hausdorff dimension, topological dimension, self-similarity, scaling laws, parameter sensitivity)
4. Strange attractors with non-integer Hausdorff dimension (Lorenz, Henon, Rossler)
5. Complex dynamics: iteration of complex functions, Julia sets, Fatou sets, parameter spaces
6. Measurement tools: box-counting dimension, similarity dimension, lacunarity, Lyapunov exponents
7. Rendering methods for fractals: escape-time coloring, orbit traps, distance estimation, domain coloring
8. Visual behavior: branching, filaments, dust, bulbs, self-similarity modes, zoom behavior
9. Shader and generative art implementation of fractal algorithms (GLSL, p5.js, TouchDesigner)
10. AI prompt vocabulary specific to fractal structure and behavior
11. Symbolic rewrite systems that produce fractal-dimension outputs (L-systems, substitution systems)
12. Fractal dimension applications: coastlines, terrain, vascular systems, antenna design
13. Multifractals: systems with multiple scaling exponents
14. 3D fractals: Mandelbulb, Mandelbox, quaternion Julia sets
15. Kleinian limit sets and their fractal boundaries

---

## Out of Scope

The following are explicitly out of scope:

1. **Perlin and Simplex noise** unless specifically used to construct fractional Brownian motion surfaces with analyzed fractal dimension
2. **Sacred geometry** (Flower of Life, Metatron's Cube, etc.) unless a specific fractal dimension analysis is provided
3. **Arbitrary tilings and tessellations** (Penrose, Wang tiles) unless fractal dimension is involved
4. **Cellular automata** in general — only those that produce fractal-dimension outputs (e.g., Rule 90 / Sierpinski) are in scope
5. **Chaos theory** in general — only the intersection where chaotic systems produce fractal structure (strange attractors, Julia set boundaries)
6. **Reaction-diffusion patterns** (Turing patterns, Belousov-Zhabotinsky) unless fractal dimension is demonstrated
7. **Generic recursive art** without measurable self-similar or fractal-dimension properties
8. **Statistical self-similarity without dimension analysis** — "it looks fractal" is not sufficient
9. **Sound design and music** unless directly connected to fractal structure (e.g., 1/f noise, fractal waveform generation)
10. **Pure topology** not connected to fractal dimension or iterative geometry

---

## Boundary Cases

Some topics sit on the boundary and require judgment:

- **Voronoi diagrams**: Out of scope unless the generator distribution or boundary structure has fractal dimension
- **Diffusion-limited aggregation (DLA)**: In scope — DLA produces structures with measurable fractal dimension (~1.71 in 2D)
- **Random walks**: In scope when analyzing fractal dimension of paths; out of scope for probabilistic analysis not connected to dimension
- **Algorithmic art**: Out of scope unless the algorithm specifically produces fractal-dimension outputs
- **Mandala and symmetric patterns**: Out of scope unless generated by fractal processes (IFS, substitution)

When a boundary case is included, the entry must explicitly state the fractal dimension and why the subject qualifies.

---

## The Drift Test

Before adding any content, apply this test:

1. Does this object or process have a measurable or estimable Hausdorff dimension that is non-integer, OR a rigorously defined self-similar structure?
2. Is it generated by iteration, recursion, rewriting, or a stochastic process with fractal-dimension outputs?
3. Would removing this content leave a gap in understanding fractals as a mathematical and generative domain?

If the answer to all three is no, the content does not belong here.
